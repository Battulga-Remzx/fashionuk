Victorian Women's Fashion Vocabulary

BONNETa women's hat tied under the chin - эмэгтэй малгай
SLEEVESparts of a garment that cover the arms - ханцуй
LACEa delicate decorative fabric - тор
PETTICOATa skirt worn under a dress - дотуур банзал
EMBROIDERYdecorative stitching on fabric - хатгамал
PARASOLa light umbrella for sun protection - нарны шүхэр
SILHOUETTEthe shape or outline of clothing - хувцасны хэлбэр
PATENT LEATHERshiny leather material - гялгар арьс
WHALEBONEa stiff material used in corsets - халимны яс
RIBBONSnarrow strips of fabric for decoration - тууз
PAGODA SLEEVESwide sleeves that open at the end - өргөн амсартай ханцуй

Parasol

Did you know? Queen Victoria popularized the white wedding dress in 1840, a tradition that continues today! Victorian women often wore ribbons in their hair and carried parasols to protect their pale skin.

BOWLER HAThard felt hat with rounded crown - дугуй малгай
TAILCOATformal coat with long tails at back - урт сүүлтэй хүрэм
WAISTCOATsleeveless garment worn over shirt - хантааз

Shoes
Leather boots and Oxford shoes for formal wear
Accessories
Cravat, pocket watch, and walking cane
Hats
Top hat and bowler hat - essential headwear
Coats
Tailcoat and frock coat for formal occasions
Shirts & Waistcoats
High-collared shirts with elegant waistcoats
Trousers
Straight or slightly fitted formal trousers

Did you know? Pocket watches were often attached to a chain called a "fob" and were considered essential for any gentleman. Victorian men wore cravats as early neckties and carried walking canes as status symbols.
